The Thadingyut Festival

Lit candles, play firecracks, release hot air balloons and join our festival for happy folks

01

What is Thadingyut?

The Thadingyut Festival, which is also known as the Lighting Festival of Myanmar is celebrated on the full moon day of the Burmese lunar month of Thadingyut. It is held at the end of the Buddhist sabbath as a tradition. After Thingyan, it is the second most well-known festival in Myanmar. The Thadingyut Festival marks the Buddha's arrival on earth after he has preached the Abhidhamma to his mother, Maya, who has been reborn in heaven. The end of the Buddhist sabbath occurs in Thadingyut, the seventh month of the Myanmar calendar. The full moon day, the day after the full moon day, and the day before the full moon day are all included in the festival's three-day duration.

02

History of Thadingyut

Buddha's mother, Maya, died seven days after the Buddha was born, and then she was reborn in the Trayastrimsa Heaven as a male deva. In order to show gratitude for motherhood, Buddha preached Abhidhamma to that deva, who was his mother for three Lenten months. When he was descending back to the mortal world, the ruler of the Trayastrimsa Heaven ordered all the saints and evils to make three precious stairways. Those stairways were made of gold, silver, and rubies. The Buddha took the middle one with the ruby. The Devas came from the right golden stairways and the Brahmas from the left silver stairways.
